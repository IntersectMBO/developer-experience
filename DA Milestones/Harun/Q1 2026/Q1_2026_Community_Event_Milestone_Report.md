## Achievement Summary

I attended four Web3 events during Q1 2026. Events spanned a local community meetup, a large-scale technology conference, an industry networking evening, and a Cardano-specific summit, with three of the four taking place during the same Nairobi tech week in February.

---

## Events Attended

### 1. Cardano Hub Nairobi — January Monthly Meetup

**Date:** January 29, 2026
**Location:** Blockchain Centre NBO, Nairobi, Kenya
**Format:** In-Person Community Meetup

The monthly Cardano Hub Nairobi meetup drew a strong turnout of attendees interested in learning about Cardano. The event provided a recurring space for education, networking, and ecosystem updates, welcoming both newcomers and established community members.

---

### 2. Africa Tech Summit

**Date:** February 10–11, 2026
**Location:** Nairobi, Kenya
**Format:** In-Person Conference
**Website:** [africatechsummit.com/nairobi](https://www.africatechsummit.com/nairobi/)

Africa Tech Summit is one of Africa's flagship technology conferences, bringing together innovators, investors, and builders from across the continent and beyond. Cardano participated as one of many ecosystems, represented at a dedicated Cardano corner alongside the Cardano Foundation and other ecosystem organisations.

Stablecoins and cross-border payments emerged as the dominant theme throughout the event, reflecting the strong grassroots adoption of digital assets, particularly stablecoin-based peer-to-peer transactions, across African markets. The summit provided an important setting to represent Intersect and the Developer Experience program to a broad audience of tech professionals.

---

### 3. Sunset & Stablecoins — Anzens, BlockAi & Encryptus

**Date:** February 11, 2026 (7:00 PM – 9:30 PM)
**Location:** Nairobi, Kenya
**Format:** In-Person Industry Networking Evening

This industry evening brought together professionals and innovators working on digital assets in Africa and beyond, with a focus on stablecoin adoption, real-world asset (RWA) tokenization, and blockchain infrastructure. A highlight was an address by Shantnoo, Acting CEO of Anzens, who spoke on the intersection of mobile money and stablecoins, exploring how stablecoin infrastructure can extend the reach of digital payments to underserved populations across Africa.

---

### 4. Cardano Africa Tech Summit (CATS)

**Date:** February 13, 2026
**Location:** Nairobi, Kenya
**Format:** In-Person Conference
**Website:** [cats.wada.org](https://cats.wada.org/)

The Cardano Africa Tech Summit, sponsored by WADA and the Blockchain Center Nairobi, was the standout event of the quarter. Despite a challenging market environment, attendance exceeded expectations with the venue at capacity, a strong signal of growing Cardano adoption and community maturity in the region.

CATS brought together a diverse gathering of Cardano community members, developers, advocates, and institutional representatives. Organisations present included the Cardano Foundation, Intersect, Emurgo, MLabs, CVVC, UNDP, and government representatives, reflecting broad interest in Cardano's potential across multiple sectors.

This was also a personal milestone: the first time all four members of the current Intersect Developer Advocate cohort met in person. Dan Baruka led a developer deep-dive masterclass at the summit, guiding attendees through a pathway from zero blockchain knowledge to active Cardano contribution. The audience was enthusiastic, though the most consistent barrier identified was not knowing where to begin.

A standout session was a breakout discussion facilitated by Adam Dean, Sam Leathers, and Kyle, centred on what problems Africans would want to solve using Cardano. The session reinforced a theme that ran throughout the summit: African builders are ready to solve African problems on their own terms, and what is most needed is accessible onboarding, funding pathways, and distribution support rather than prescriptive direction from outside the continent.

---

## Key Themes

Stablecoins and mobile money were the dominant conversation at Africa Tech Summit and the Sunset & Stablecoins evening, with adoption across African markets, particularly in peer-to-peer transactions, already a present reality rather than a future aspiration.

At CATS, the conversation was broader. Education emerged as the principal barrier to Cardano adoption across multiple sessions: attendees were eager to build but consistently uncertain where to start. Distribution was identified as an equally critical and underemphasised challenge: how builders get products into the hands of the people who need them. The Cardano Hubs model was highlighted as a scalable approach to grassroots community building, with calls for replication across additional African regions.

---

## Conclusion

Q1 2026 was a strong quarter for community engagement. The February tech week in Nairobi created a concentrated period of meaningful interaction with both the broader African tech ecosystem and the core Cardano community. The energy and turnout at CATS in particular reinforced that Cardano's fundamentals continue to attract committed builders, and that the Developer Advocate program is directly addressing the most commonly raised need: guided onboarding for developers who are ready to contribute.
