## Overview

A regional in-person workshop was held at the Blockchain Center Nairobi on 14 February 2026, co-organised by the Intersect Developer Advocate team the day after CATS. The session targeted East African web developers new to Cardano, with invitations crafted and distributed by me to reach developers outside the existing Cardano community.

---

## Workshop Details

**Name:** Cardano Layered Architecture Deep Dive: A Workshop for Builders  
**Date:** 14 February 2026  
**Location:** Blockchain Center Nairobi, Nairobi, Kenya  
**Format:** In-Person Regional Workshop (90 minutes, 3 developers, 30 mins each, live code and demos)  
**Organised by:** Dan Baruka, Emmanuel, Uche, and Harun Waweru Mwangi  
**Target Audience:** Web developers new to Cardano, open-source contributors, and teams seeking architectural clarity  
**My Role:** Designed and distributed the workshop invitations, handling community outreach.

The workshop walked participants through how Intersect's core repositories map to the Cardano Blueprint, covering cardano-base and lsm-tree at the protocol layer, ouroboros-network, cardano-node, and cardano-cli at the network and application layers, and how the specifications connect to the implementation.

Turnout was lower than anticipated because of the long tech week. The session was recorded live to extend access to developers who could not attend in person.

---

## Regional Impact

The Blockchain Center Nairobi is one of the few dedicated blockchain learning spaces in East Africa, where 75% of Kenya's population is under 35. Hosting in-region, with an outreach-first invitation approach targeting newcomers rather than existing Cardano developers, directly addressed access and representation gaps. The DA team co-organising the workshop spanned Kenya, Nigeria, and Mauritius, reflecting pan-African representation.

The CATS Fireside Discussion recorded on the same day reinforced this further, with all four DAs sharing reflections on African developer inclusion, the diversity of CATS attendees, and the case for expanding regional hubs across the continent.
