## Summary

During Q1 2026, I led two Developer Experience Working Group sessions as part of the Q1 "Deepening Engagement" series. Sessions focused on showcasing non-coding community contributions and establishing documentation best practices for open source Cardano projects. I maintained the session cadence, facilitated community discussion, and produced comprehensive documentation for each session hosted on the DevEx portal.

## Sessions Led

### Session 09: Community Builder Story – Konma & HaskLedger (2026/02/05)

A spotlight session featuring **Vinay Devabhakthuni** (Konma Lab) and **Bernard Sibanda**, exploring how non-coding contributions drive ecosystem growth in Cardano. Vinay presented Konma's education-to-employment model and their work on HaskLedger, a verification layer for Haskell-based ledgers, while Bernard shared his strategy for scaling Cardano adoption across Africa. The session highlighted the impact of community builders, educators, and advocates beyond pure engineering work.

### Session 11: Open Source & Documentation

A session focused on the principles and practice of effective open source documentation. Topics covered included Documentation Driven Development, the ROI of well-maintained documentation, document types (READMEs, Design Docs, Executable Docs, Contributor Guidelines), and documentation formats (Markdown, LaTeX, Typst, AsciiDoc, Mermaid diagrams). The session also addressed AI-assisted documentation tooling, localization considerations, and included a community discussion on the challenges of centralizing and maintaining Cardano developer resources.

## Developer Experience Pain Points

### 1. Community Builder Visibility

**Solution:** The Community Builder Story session surfaced non-coding contribution models and gave visibility to builders working on education and ecosystem scaling. This helps newcomers see pathways into Cardano beyond software development.

### 2. Documentation Quality and Discoverability

**Solution:** The Open Source & Documentation session introduced structured documentation frameworks and showcased best-in-class Cardano repositories, giving contributors clear templates and standards to apply to Core Cardano repositories.

### 3. AI-Driven Documentation Uncertainty

**Solution:** Community discussion during Session 11 surfaced real-world insights on AI documentation tooling, versioning challenges, and what automation can and cannot replace, helping contributors approach AI-assisted docs with appropriate expectations.
