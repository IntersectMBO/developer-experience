## Introduction 1: ACSAP-K and the Cardano Ecosystem

**Cardano Project:** Intersect / Blockchain Center Nairobi  
**Potential Collaborator:** Association of County Students Association Presidents – Kenya (ACSAP-K)  
**Contact:** Kahindi James  
**Initial Introduction:** 13 February 2026, Cardano Africa Tech Summit, Nairobi  
**Follow-up Meeting:** 24 February 2026, Google Meet  
**Status:** Early-stage, follow-up in progress

ACSAP-K is a national student leadership organisation representing over 600,000 university students across Kenyan counties. At CATS, Kahindi James was introduced to the Cardano ecosystem and connected directly to Darlington, President of the Blockchain Center Nairobi, establishing a bridge between Intersect's community infrastructure and one of Kenya's largest student networks.

The follow-up meeting with Kahindi explored pathways for IntersectMBO to engage with ACSAP-K's membership through blockchain education, Intersect student membership, working group participation, and future outreach initiatives. The scale of ACSAP-K's reach across Kenyan counties makes this a significant channel for long-term grassroots adoption.

## Introduction 2: TxMeta and Dairy Chain

**Cardano Project:** TxMeta  
**Potential Collaborator:** Dairy Chain (agri-tech startup)  
**Context:** Exploratory introduction to evaluate Cardano as the foundation for a milk traceability system  
**Status:** Early-stage, discussions ongoing

Dairy Chain is a Kenyan agri-tech startup exploring blockchain-based traceability for milk supply chains, covering sourcing, processing, and distribution. As a developer at TxMeta, a Cardano-focused development company, I facilitated an introductory meeting between the two parties to explore whether TxMeta could support building this system on Cardano.

The conversation covered smart contract capabilities, transaction costs, and available Cardano tooling for supply chain applications. The meeting surfaced a concrete, locally relevant use case for Cardano in African agri-tech. No formal collaboration has been established and discussions are ongoing.
